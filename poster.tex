  {\Large\bfseries\color{mutedText}Part I $\cdot$ Probing from the outside}\par
  \vspace{3mm}

  % ─── Q1 KNOCKOUT ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accBlue]
    \qtitle{Q1 $\cdot$ Do all 16 heads matter equally?}
    No. For each author I kept \emph{one} head's LoRA weights and zeroed the other 15, then measured how much of the adapter's perplexity improvement the single head recovers.
    \textbf{\color{accBlue}H11} leads for \textbf{51/77} authors.
    \textbf{\color{accRed}H14} leads for \textbf{18} elevated-register writers: Homer, Milton, Poe.
    \textbf{\color{accGreen}H3} almost never leads but consistently ranks high. The universal backup.

    \vspace{3mm}
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/knockout_best_head.png}

    \vspace{2mm}
    \textbf{Fine-tuning concentrates, it doesn't distribute.} And the clustering by register (fairy tale $\to$ H11, elevated $\to$ H14) holds across 77 independently trained adapters $-$ so these heads were already specialists before Carroll or Poe existed.
  \end{qpanel}

  % ─── Q2 STEER ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accBlue]
    \qtitle{Q2 $\cdot$ Can I steer a head like a dial?}
    Scale head outputs at inference, $0\times$ to $2\times$.
    \begin{quotebox}
      \qlabel{Carroll, H11 at $1\times$:} ``Alice asked her, `Why, dark and I am dark. I am inside the clouds\ldots'\,''\\[1mm]
      \qlabel{H11 at $0\times$ (killed):} ``The little rabbit, the storm was very bad\ldots The rabbit wanted to go to the rabbit's home\ldots''
    \end{quotebox}
    Kill the dominant head and perplexity on author text spikes; the quote above is one visible sample. The chart shows the same mirror asymmetry across the eval corpus. Surprise: LoRA changes \textbf{what} heads output, not \textbf{where} they look $-$ and it concentrates the change into one head, not sixteen.

    \vspace{2mm}
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/steering_contrast.png}
  \end{qpanel}

  % ─── Q3 TRANSPLANT ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accPurple]
    \qtitle{Q3 $\cdot$ Can I transplant a head from one author to another?}
    Take Minimalist, swap in Poe's H14, 6\% of the LoRA weights.
    \begin{quotebox}
      \qlabel{Minimalist:} ``The trees began to tremble. The people were scared.''\\[1mm]
      \qlabel{+ Poe's H14:} ``Lightning flashed and thunder made me weep\ldots the thunder roared past her\ldots''
    \end{quotebox}
    Short sentences stay. Poe's dark vocabulary floods in.
  \end{qpanel}

  % ─── Q4 BLEND ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accBlack]
    \qtitle{\demo Q4 $\cdot$ Can I blend two authors?}
    Linear interpolation: $(1{-}\alpha)\times$ \textbf{\color{accBlue}Carroll} $+\;\alpha\times$ \textbf{\color{accPurple}Poet}.

    \vspace{2mm}

    \textbf{LoRA weight space isn't a style space.} Some paths are smooth, others aren't.

  % ═══ PART II banner ═══
  {\Large\bfseries\color{mutedText}Part II $\cdot$ Looking inside}\par
  \vspace{3mm}

  % ─── Q5 SAE / HEAD ROLES ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accGreen]
    \qtitle{Q5 $\cdot$ What do these heads actually compute?}
    Trained a \textbf{sparse autoencoder} on the residual stream. $\sim$25 interpretable features emerge. Each fires on a specific pattern:

    \vspace{2mm}
    \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/sae_token_heatmap_article_v2.png}
    \vspace{1mm}

    {\color{featPink}\textbf{First-person ``I''}} fires on the pronoun. {\color{featAmber}\textbf{Dialogue}} fires on \textit{said}, quote marks, speaker-attribution patterns. Others: verse line-breaks, sentence-ending periods, archaic pronouns, dark atmosphere.

    Matching features back to the 16 heads gives each head a role:
    {\color{accGreen}\textbf{H3}} reads everything.
    {\color{accBlue}\textbf{H11}} reads alone. Opaque to the SAE.
    {\color{accRed}\textbf{H14}} is a formality enforcer.
    {\color{accOrange}\textbf{MLP}} holds emergent directions no head controls.
  \end{qpanel}

  % ─── Q6 FEATURE STEERING / COMPOSE ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accPurple]
    \qtitle{\demo Q6 $\cdot$ Can I steer with individual features?}
    Same prompt, same Carroll adapter. Flip one SAE feature at a time:
    \begin{quotebox}
      \qlabel{baseline:} ``and she came across a tiny little little voice. The bunny hopped along\ldots Alice took him in the little house and said, `Oh, I wonder\ldots'\,''\\[1mm]
      \qlabel{\color{featTeal}+ simplicity:} ``She looked up{\color{featTeal}.} It was a little sad{\color{featTeal}.} The cat had been up{\color{featTeal}.} It was very long{\color{featTeal}.} The little cat went up the stairs{\color{featTeal}.}\,''\\[1mm]
      \qlabel{\color{featPink}+ first-person ``I'':} ``\,{\color{featPink}\textbf{I}} hope {\color{featPink}\textbf{I}} can't think like what will happen next. In hope {\color{featPink}\textbf{I}} will give hope that will like it can have\ldots''\\[1mm]
      \qlabel{\color{featAmber}+ dialogue:} ``\,`That's very sad,' she {\color{featAmber}\textbf{said}}. `I'm sorry,' {\color{featAmber}\textbf{said}} the King. `Let's the King.' {\color{featAmber}\textbf{cried}}\ldots''\\[1mm]
      \qlabel{\color{featSlate}+ verse:} ``very that she was now the farmer\ldots\\ \textit{``You've been the day was getting so bad,''}\\ \ldots''
    \end{quotebox}

    \vspace{2mm}
    \begin{tcolorbox}[enhanced, arc=2mm,
      colback=highlightBg, colframe=highlightBg, boxrule=0pt,
      left=4mm, right=4mm, top=1.5mm, bottom=1.5mm,
      before skip=0mm, after skip=0mm]
      {\normalsize\demo Pick an author, drag the simplicity slider, watch the voice change.}
    \end{tcolorbox}
  \end{qpanel}

  % ─── Q7 DETECTION ≠ STEERING ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accOrange]
    \qtitle{Q7 $\cdot$ Does detection equal steering?}
    \textbf{No.} Archaic-pronoun features fire perfectly on ``thou'' in Blake and Milton. But injecting them produces nothing archaic. The model collapses first.

    \textbf{Perfect detectors, useless steering vectors.} Steering amplifies only what the model can already express.
  \end{qpanel}

  % ─── Q8 CONCLUSION ───
  \begin{qpanel}[accRed]
    \qtitle{Q8 $\cdot$ Where does the style live in this model?}
    {\bfseries Style lives in two layers.}

    \vspace{1mm}
    {\color{accBlue}\textbf{BELOW (attention)}}: one pre-existing specialist head per author carries most of the LoRA's perplexity improvement. Fine-tuning concentrates, it doesn't distribute.

    \vspace{1mm}
    {\color{accGreen}\textbf{ABOVE (residual stream)}}: universal \textbf{structural} knobs (simplicity, dialogue, verse) steer on \emph{any} adapter; author-specific \textbf{semantic} fingerprints (dark atmosphere, cozy food, dialect) detect everywhere but only steer with the matching adapter.

    \vspace{1mm}
    LoRAs \textbf{amplify}, they don't create. And the strongest style direction lives in the \textbf{\color{accOrange}MLP}, invisible to any head.
  \end{qpanel}
